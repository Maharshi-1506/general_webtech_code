Natural Formation: A majestic, towering landform created by geological processes over millions of years. Large Scale: Encompasses a vast area, with varying heights, slopes, and valleys. Material: Composed primarily of rock, soil, and vegetation. Static: Generally remains fixed in position, with minimal changes over time. Purpose: Provides habitat for various plants and animals, influences weather patterns, and offers recreational opportunities.

Man-Made Structure: Built by humans for shelter, living, and other purposes. Small Scale: Relatively small in comparison to a mountain, with defined boundaries. Materials: Constructed using various materials like wood, bricks, concrete, and stone. Dynamic: Can be modified, repaired, or demolished over time. Purpose: Provides a living space for people, offering comfort, protection, and a sense of belonging.
